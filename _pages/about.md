I'm an economist and MSc student in Economics at the Universidad de Chile, with applied research experience in experimental economics, microeconomics, and public opinion, focused on quantitative methods and the use of large language models (LLMs) for social research.

I currently work as a Project Research Analyst at the Centre for Experimental Social Sciences (CESS), a centre associated with Nuffield College, University of Oxford, and Universidad de Santiago de Chile (USACH), and on *Talking to Machines*, a separate project sponsored by Nuffield College, University of Oxford.

My research interests center on inequalities, vulnerable populations, and crime.

Research interests
======
Inequalities, vulnerable populations, and crime.

Selected projects
======
* **[CANDOUR](https://oxford-candour.com/)** — Cross-national survey (Duch lab, Nuffield, Oxford) on distributive preferences and welfare during the COVID-19 pandemic; co-authored papers in *PNAS* (2021) and *PLoS Medicine* (2023).
* **[Talking to Machines](https://talkingtomachines.org/)** — IDB + University of Oxford project building LLM-based digital twins of Latin American individuals to study behavioral economics and social preferences (tax compliance, corruption tolerance, institutional trust, redistribution).
