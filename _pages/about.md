I'm an economist and MSc student in Economics at the Universidad de Chile, with applied research experience in experimental economics, microeconomics, and public opinion, focused on quantitative methods and the use of large language models (LLMs) for social research.

I currently work as a Project Research Analyst at the Centre for Experimental Social Sciences (CESS), a centre associated with Nuffield College, University of Oxford, and Universidad de Santiago de Chile (USACH), and on *Talking to Machines*, a separate project sponsored by Nuffield College, University of Oxford.

My research interests center on inequalities, vulnerable populations, and crime.

Research interests
======
Inequalities, vulnerable populations, and crime.

Selected projects
======
* **[CANDOUR](https://oxford-candour.com/)** — Global research collaboration (Oxford Population Health, Nuffield College, and international university partners) using experimental methods to study health, economic, and political policy challenges, initiated in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. My work focused on its cross-national survey on distributive preferences and welfare during the pandemic, resulting in co-authored papers in *PNAS* (2021) and *PLoS Medicine* (2023).
* **[Talking to Machines](https://talkingtomachines.org/)** — International research initiative applying AI and large language models to experimental social science. My work spans several of its projects:
  * **LADT** — LLM-based digital twins of Latin American individuals, with the Inter-American Development Bank, studying behavioral economics and social preferences (tax compliance, corruption tolerance, institutional trust, redistribution).
  * **AIPOP (Chile)** — Comparing human survey responses with LLM-predicted responses inferred from social media activity, to validate LLMs as a tool for public-opinion research.
  * **Market Signals (Finfluencers)** — Querying LLM-based digital twins of financial influencers for daily stock and macro views, testing whether the resulting signals predict equity returns.
  * **Synthetic Replication Games** — Replicating published behavioral-science studies by substituting LLM agents for human subjects, comparing synthetic results against the original human findings.
