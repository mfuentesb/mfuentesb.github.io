Matías Fuentes Becerra is an economist and MSc student in Economics at the Universidad de Chile, with applied research experience in experimental economics, microeconomics, and public opinion, focused on quantitative methods and the use of large language models (LLMs) for social research.

He currently works as a Project Research Analyst at the Centre for Experimental Social Sciences (CESS), a centre associated with Nuffield College, University of Oxford, and Universidad de Santiago de Chile (USACH), and on *Talking to Machines*, a separate project sponsored by Nuffield College, University of Oxford.

His research interests center on inequalities, vulnerable populations, and crime. He has worked on projects spanning police pacification and education in Brazil, distributive preferences during the COVID-19 pandemic, consumer-credit information frictions, and LLM-based digital twins of Latin American survey respondents and financial influencers.

Research interests
======
Inequalities, vulnerable populations, and crime.

Selected projects
======
* **Police pacification and education (Rio de Janeiro)** — Master's thesis on the effect of Rio de Janeiro's UPP police-pacification program on ENEM (Brazilian national high-school exam) scores and participation at nearby schools, using heterogeneity-robust difference-in-differences / event-study methods.
* **CANDOUR** — Cross-national survey (Duch lab, Nuffield, Oxford) on distributive preferences and welfare during the COVID-19 pandemic; co-authored papers in *PNAS* (2021) and *PLoS Medicine* (2023).
* **Consumer-credit price comparison (RCT)** — Randomized trial studying how information frictions in consumer credit produce unequal loan terms.
* **Talking to Machines (LADT)** — IDB + University of Oxford project building LLM-based digital twins of Latin American individuals to study behavioral economics and social preferences (tax compliance, corruption tolerance, institutional trust, redistribution).
* **FinFluencer** — LLM-based digital twins of financial influencers, queried to generate equity-return forecasts and trading signals.
